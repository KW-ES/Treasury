Types of Funding

Source[1]

Overview

RMB is transitioning from cash-based TS Account's (Chameleon) to a trade-based funding model in Murex.

Under the new framework, all portfolios are fully funded by Group Treasury (GTSY) using three processes

Three Methods

  1. Trade Funding
  2. Position Funding
  3. Residual Funding

These methods ensure that every trading and banking position is funded at the correct cost, using the appropriate FTP curve.

1. Matched Maturity Funding

AKA Trade Funding

Concept

  • Real-time, one-to-one matching of trades with funding.
  • Each trade generates an internal loan/deposit with matching maturity and cashflow profile.
  • Priced off the FTP Curve
    • ()

Example - Bond Purchase

  1. Trader buys R 100 mil SA Gov Bond, maturity
  2. To pay the seller, GTSY provides R 100 M loan with maturity.
  3. FTP Rate = 10Y swap + liquidity premium (say 9%)
  4. Bonds Desk reconds:
    • Asset: R 100 m bond
    • Liability: R 100 m FTP loan at 9%

→ The Desk can only make money if the bond return is greater than 9%.

2. Position Funding

AKA Behavioural Funding

Concept

  • End-of-day batch process.
  • Funds open positions where no clear contractual maturity exists
    • equities,
    • revolving loans,
    • unsettled capital
  • Treasury applies a behavioural tenor (e.g. 1M, 3M) to approximate how long the position ties up liquidity.
  • Rolled daily, but priced at the behavioural FTP Curve.

Example — Equities Desk

  1. Equities Desk holds R 200 m of listed shares.
  2. That R200 m still required cash to buy the shares
    • → Treasury provided it.
  3. But equities have no maturity date. Treasury assumes they behave like 1M rolling funding.
  4. At EOD, system books:
    • Internal funding loan: R200m at .
    • Asset: R 200 m equities.
    • Liability: R 200 m behavioural funding loan.

→ Desk only shows profit if equity returns is greater than cost of rolling 1M funding.

3. Residual Cash Funding

AKA Overnight Sweep

Concept

  • Final sweep1 at end of day.
  • Captures any remaining cash balances not already funded.
  • Always priced at the Overnight FTP rate (repo/OIS).
  • Typically covers realised P&L and timing mismatches.

Example — FX Desk Profit

  1. FX Desk realises ZAR 5 m profit today.
  2. At EOD, system sweeps it into GTSY as an overnight deposit.
  3. Desk earns FTP (O/N) until it redeploys the cash.

→ Ensures no stray balances remain in portfolios.

Sequence of Processes

  1. Trade Funding → runs in real time as trades are booked.
  2. Position Funding → runs at EOD to capture behavioural positions.
  3. Residual Funding → runs last at EOD to sweep any leftovers.

At the end of each day, all portfolios are fully funded and all liquidity sits with Treasury.

Why Funding is Always Needed

Even if the desk “already has the asset”:

  • To buy an asset, you needed cash → Treasury provided it.
  • That cash has a cost → reflected via an FTP loan.
  • Without this, desks would appear to make “free” returns, ignoring liquidity cost.

Funding ensures true profitability is visible.
